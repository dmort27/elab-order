how leader work. A few comments may be given on ways in which leaders
work. As we have seen, people may be encouraged to lollowers a given
course of action by a leader who helps them to see that it is to their
interest todo so. It is still better if he can cause these followers
to feel that the line of action proposed is one they themselves have
chosen.
It can be seen that such a leader might be just a damagogue-one who
does whatever the crowed seems to prefer, regardless of truth or
values. The good leader, however, will select those values toward
whivh he wishes to see progress made, and direct the attenion of his
followers toward them.
The followers must secure satisfactions which will make them want to
co-operate. The leader must be able to show his followers that they
will derive some benefit from the course of action he is proposing.
