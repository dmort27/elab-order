It seems to me that there exists a tendency for some in our community
to "txhim kho Hmoob" as if they are the only ones who somehow hold the
key to solving some "Hmong problem."
Why doesn't anyone first asked the Hmong community (conduct a survey
or some measurable process, or something) as to whether the Hmong
community would like to be subjectded to some arbitrary "txhim kho"
effort, or, whether they even have these dire "Hmong problems" to
begin with?
Why do some of us assume so much about what our community really
desires, and therefore the slew of proposals to "fix" our "problems,"
as if we are openly soliciting your great intellectual minds, skill
sets, to carry the day for us?
Isn't that how things work in the real world of the Hmong people,
especially today in 21s Century America? You are lawyer, I have legal
problems, I come to you. You are a doctor, I have medical problems, I
came to you. You are a professor, if I need a class to fulfill my
studies, I sign up for your class. Likewise, if I have something you
need, you will come and seek my services and expertise.
Have some decent respect for the dignity and intelligence and common
sense of the Hmong community in general!
But, why all these assumptions about what people need and want? And,
why the elitism?
Additionally, it seems to be that there exists a tendency for some in
our community to focus so much attention on our people's problems,
short comings, deficiencies, divisions, and other negatives that don't
add very much to the positive and much needed discussions of the
promise of America, the ideas of true democracy and mainstream,
legitimate political involvement, the search for true equality for
all, the promise of justice and freedom, the reality of an
interdependent and global community, and....the celebration of the
great achievements and creative ideas and innovations of our people to
fully partake in the shaping of our own future...as we have done,
doing, and will no doubt continue to do!
It's not what we do, it's HOW we do it. The same tired ways of
addressing these same issues, top-down style, needs to stop.
Can we for once celebrate, support, enhance and add to the vastly
diverse, truly rich and ever so vibrant soul that makes us Hmong? The
spirit of the Hmong has never seen a brighter day, has never seen or
taste freedom and opportunity, has never known the limits and endless
possibilities of our potential...as we do right now in the spectrum of
our entire history! Ever!
And believe it or not, YOU committee folks are a part of that
celebration of achievements. So, stop pandering to the gloom and doom
conversations that leads only to pettiness and political score keeping
in our community about who's who. As much as you all think of
yourselves as selfless passionate advocates for Hmong people, it's
inevitable that your egos, personal or professonal, are on full
display.
