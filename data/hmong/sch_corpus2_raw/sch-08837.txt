many people seems to think that the hmong new year celebration yog ua
tom tshav puam.  temsis sawvdaws tsi paub tias hmoob ua dabtsi tom tshav
pum tiag.  hmong new year doens't necessarily mean "tom tshav puam" xwb.
hmong can skip such an even and still celebrate hmong new year inhouse.
luag tej laus tias "tsa hauv toj".  nco ntsoov tias hmoob hu plig 30 on
new year's eve only.  cov ua do it before and after ces..yog tsi paub es
lam ua lawm xwb...or yog tias lawd ua before or after new year's eve
ces...yog muaj lwm yam los tab kaum xwb.  noj tsiab is always on new
years eve or new years day xwb...not after.
temsis hais txog tsa hauv toj ces...we do it beginning on new years day
and usually last a few days.  temsis yog tsi muaj ncej ntxheb/ntxhoo ces
hu tsi tau ua "tsa hauv toj".
the story behind "tsa hauv toj" relates to "vajhmoob" lawm.  puag thaum
ub, tom qab uas hmoob fuabtais tuag lawm....hmoob xij niaj xyoo mus tsa
hauv toj..hoping that kev mob yuav mus....and kev zoo los yog kev nom
tswv yuav los.  for this reason, hmoob thiaj li choose toj roob hauv pes
los tsa hauv toj xwb (or somewhere close to mountainous areas), seb looj
mem puas qhib...seb hauv toj puas tawm plawg vajhmoob los...
yaweh
